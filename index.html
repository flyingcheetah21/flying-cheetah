Monitoring Land at Scale Is Increasingly Difficult

Large agricultural operations and conservation areas face a growing challenge: identifying plant stress, disease, and environmental disruption early enough to act.


Traditional inspections are labor-intensive, reactive, and often too late to prevent meaningful loss. By the time symptoms are visible from the ground, impact has already occurred.

Early Detection. Actionable Intelligence.

Sylva Systems deploys unmanned aerial systems equipped with high-resolution imaging to monitor vegetation health across large areas efficiently.


Through systematic analysis of aerial data, we identify subtle patterns of stress and irregular growth before they become visible to the naked eye.


Our mission is to provide land managers with clear, data-driven insight that protects yield, biodiversity, and long-term ecosystem stability.

How It Works

Deploy

Scheduled aerial surveys capture high-resolution imagery across farmland and protected landscapes.

Analyze

Imagery is processed to identify anomalies and early indicators of plant stress or disease.

Act

Clients receive visual reports highlighting priority zones for proactive intervention.
